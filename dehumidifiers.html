How to Choose the Right Size (Beginner-Friendly)

Ignore marketing buzzwords and focus on these 3 things:

  1. How big is the space? A small basement can still be very damp—size alone isn’t enough.
  2. How wet is it? Musty smell or visible damp spots usually means you need more capacity.
  3. Where will the water go? If you can run a hose to a drain/sump, do it. Bucket-emptying gets old fast.
Target humidity: Most homes feel best at 45–50% in basements. If you go much lower, you’ll waste energy. Much higher, and mold risk rises.

Common Mistakes (Avoid These)

  • Buying too small: It will run nonstop and still won’t hit target humidity.
  • No drain plan: If you rely on the bucket, you’ll eventually stop using it.
  • Setting humidity too low: Wastes energy and can make the space feel chilly.
  • Ignoring airflow: Keep doors open if possible and don’t shove the unit in a tight corner.
